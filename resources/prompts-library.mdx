## How to write effective prompts

- Start with a clear subject (person, product, scene)
- Add environment and context (location, time of day, weather)
- Specify lighting (soft window light, golden hour rim light, fluorescent)
- Include camera details (lens, aperture) and composition (close-up, low angle)
- Choose style cues (film stock, color grading, grain)
- Add motion or depth cues (for videos) and negative prompts to avoid artifacts

### Template

- Subject + Environment + Lighting + Camera + Composition + Style + Details + Negative Prompts

Example:
Subject (portrait) in Environment (meadow at golden hour) with Lighting (strong backlight rim), Camera (85mm f/1.4, low angle), Composition (full-body twirl), Style (Kodak Gold 200), Details (warm anamorphic flare), Negative (no text overlays, no artifacts)
